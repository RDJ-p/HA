My Short Story

Daniel Goleman is best known for his groundbreaking work on emotional intelligence (EI), a concept that has since become a cornerstone in understanding human behavior, leadership, and personal development. Born in 1946, Goleman had an early interest in psychology and later pursued his studies at Harvard University, where he developed an interest in the emotional and psychological aspects of human life. His career as a psychologist and journalist led him to explore the deeper layers of the mind, particularly the influence of emotions on decision-making and behavior.

BEST SELLER

Contemplating inspiration

Emotional Intelligence by Daniel Goleman explores the impact of emotions on personal and professional success. Goleman argues that IQ alone is not enough for achievement; emotional intelligence (EQ)—which includes self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, and social skills—plays a crucial role. The book combines psychology, neuroscience, and real-life examples to show how improving EQ can lead to better relationships, decision-making, and leadership.
